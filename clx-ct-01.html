01
Thesis & Doctrine
The frozen position the whole project is accountable to.

The category default is a soft, top-loading camera bag that treats a laptop, audio kit, and cables as afterthoughts stuffed around the camera core. CLX-CT-01 inverts that: the load is organized around the spine first, with protected rapid access and independent compartments designed as primary structure rather than as pockets. The reframing is deliberate — it changes what the product is measured against.

Frozen doctrine

“CLX-CT-01 is not a camera backpack. It is a spine-centered creator workstation.” Six pillars hold this position. They are provisional in detail but frozen in intent — corrections may refine a pillar's spec, not remove the pillar.

05
Form Strategy
Why the envelope tapers, and what the form has to telegraph.

The taper — 180 mm depth at the lower/mid body drawing to 150 mm at the shoulder — is not styling. It pulls mass toward the wearer's center of gravity at the top of the carry, narrows the profile where the body leans into it, and signals a worn workstation rather than a hard equipment case. Corner softening (22 mm radius) reads as a molded semi-rigid shell, reinforcing the “workstation, not case” doctrine.

Portfolio note · lens honesty

Render at 35–40 mm, not 64 mm. The longer lens compresses depth and exaggerates verticality, making the 285 mm width read narrower than it is. The geometry is correct; the hero shot should not misrepresent the envelope. A lower camera height further corrects the read.

Doctrine note · internal modules omitted from exterior

The laptop tunnel (M04) and cable/audio cassette (M05) are internal modules. They are intentionally excluded from the exterior blockout, which carries a clean service mark on the front face only. This keeps the six-pillar count honest against a five-things-visible model: two pillars live inside and are specified in the component architecture, not shown on the exterior shell. Recorded so a later diff of blockout-vs-pillars does not read the absence as drift.

Fidelity boundary

At blockout fidelity the model establishes envelope, taper, axis doctrine, hatch placement, and spine width as buildable geometry. It does not validate the laptop tunnel or cassette as sealed independent volumes — those are cued, not proven — and a blockout is not an engineering check. Downstream language must say “cued,” not “validated,” for M04 and M05, and must not treat the blockout as sign-off.
